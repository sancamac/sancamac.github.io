# Retail and Shopping Signals

Sanitized public synthesis from China Mission 2026.

## Core Frame

Retail is a truth machine for AI hardware.

A store compresses the question from "is this futuristic?" into:

- What is actually for sale?
- What is demo-only?
- What has a price?
- What can be rented, returned, repaired, or supported?
- What requires an app, region, account, cloud service, identity check, or language workaround?
- Who owns after-sales?
- What happens after the first wow?

## AI Product Stores

AI product stores were treated as market microphysics:

- many products meet real customers;
- low-cost trials or rentals reveal willingness to pay;
- staff answers expose whether support is real;
- health, pet, wearable, and home devices reveal data-boundary questions;
- product feedback loops can shorten iteration if the channel is real.

## Robot Retail

Robot retail was treated as a stricter test:

- indoor demo versus shippable configuration;
- exact SKU and price behind every robot body;
- warranty geography;
- overseas shipping and battery transport;
- app region and language support;
- repair path and spare parts;
- e-stop, reset, battery, child/crowd perimeter, and failed-command handling;
- scripted, teleoperated, or autonomous behavior.

## Good Questions

- Is the price written or only spoken?
- Is the product in stock or preorder?
- Who provides warranty and repair?
- Can it be serviced outside China?
- What data does the device collect?
- Does the product work without a local app ecosystem?
- Can the staff explain firmware, returns, spare parts, and liability?
- Does rental or trial behavior convert to repeat use?

## Practical Takeaway

AI hardware becomes serious when the support layer becomes legible. The store is not the proof; the boring answers inside the store are the proof.
