# Aerospace and Quantum

Sanitized public synthesis from China Mission 2026.

## Core Frame

Aerospace and quantum were treated as sensitive, institutional, and access-limited. The public-safe layer is about approved material, public events, museums, expo booths, education devices, and commercialization signals.

## Access Realism

Public or easier layers:

- museums and science centers;
- public education products;
- open expo booths and conference talks;
- company showrooms when explicitly public;
- approved business or educational briefings.

Hard or intro-only layers:

- state aerospace entities;
- satellite digital factories;
- quantum labs and consortia;
- restricted manufacturing sites;
- university or government-linked facilities without public visitor regimes.

## Quantum Commercialization Questions

- Is the product educational, cloud/software, lab hardware, or research-only?
- What workloads are real versus demonstrative?
- What is productized today?
- What support, curriculum, developer tooling, or deployment path exists?
- Is the business model education, research infrastructure, industry optimization, or national strategic capacity?

## Aerospace and Commercial-Space Questions

- Is the public layer a museum, innovation district, expo, park, or actual manufacturing?
- What can be learned from public displays without requesting restricted access?
- What is the commercial use case: satellite internet, ground applications, manufacturing, supply chain, or data services?
- Where does AI enter: design, manufacturing, inspection, operations, autonomy, data processing, or mission planning?

## Safety Boundary

Do not ask for restricted access, lab details, process details, photos, non-public specs, or anything that resembles procurement or technical extraction without a formal context.

## Practical Takeaway

The useful public lens is commercialization: what exists outside the lab, what has educational value, what has public demos, and what can be discussed without crossing security boundaries.
