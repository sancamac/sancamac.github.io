# Robotics and Embodied AI

Public synthesis from China Mission 2026.

## Core Frame

Embodied AI is not one robot. It is a stack:

- robot bodies: humanoids, quadrupeds, arms, dexterous hands, exoskeletons, inspection and service robots;
- data: teleoperation, imitation, reinforcement learning, simulation, synthetic worlds, public datasets, and failure logs;
- components: actuators, reducers, tactile skins, hands, joints, cables, batteries, sensors, controllers, and thermal design;
- validation: scenario contests, mid-test bases, factory pilot lines, standards, CR-style testing, and real-scene evaluation;
- deployment: safety, service, spare parts, software integration, warranty, after-sales, and responsibility when a system fails.

## Useful Entities and Signals

Shanghai signals included Zhangjiang robot-data infrastructure, public humanoid training grounds, robot-data schools, humanoid company clusters, factory expos, Baoshan industrial-robot layers, and AI+manufacturing policy targets.

Hangzhou signals included Unitree, Deep Robotics, scenario competitions, national mid-test infrastructure, public scenario marketplaces, standards/certification loops, and data/simulation partnerships.

Shenzhen signals sat closer to hardware iteration, component supply, consumer-electronics productization, and quantum/education-device crossover.

Suzhou/Taicang signals sat closer to industrial manufacturing discipline: components, machining, automation, factory proof, and supplier reality.

Hefei acted as a quantum/science wildcard with possible embodied-intelligence relevance.

## Anti-Spectacle Questions

The best questions are boring:

- What counted as autonomy?
- How many resets or interventions were allowed?
- What happens when perception fails?
- Is the behavior scripted, teleoperated, learned, or truly autonomous within a defined task?
- What is logged after failure?
- Who owns the data generated by demos, stores, homes, factories, and public spaces?
- What breaks first: tactile drift, cable fatigue, heat, battery, network, actuator wear, calibration, app dependency, or service process?
- Can the system be repaired locally?
- Is the warranty geography clear?
- Does the robot integrate with MES, ERP, WMS, PLC, safety systems, or real factory processes?

## Practical Takeaway

China's embodied-AI signal is strongest when bodies, scene owners, data infrastructure, standards, components, and service channels appear together. Single-demo claims are weak evidence. Repeatable deployment evidence is the thing to watch.
